Two Functions · Nine Modules
Function 1 · Lead Identification & Qualification
Sales Operations Management
Automates initial lead assessment and data enrichment to reduce manual triage time for operations teams managing high-volume inbound inquiries.
  • M1
    Lead Scoring Engine
    Rank inbound leads by fit and purchase intent using company profile and engagement signals
  • M2
    Data Enrichment Service
    Auto-populate missing company and contact information from public sources to improve CRM completeness
  • M3
    Pipeline Deduplication
    Identify and flag duplicate leads across CRM records to prevent splits and redundant outreach
  • M4
    Activity Recommendation Engine
    Suggest next-best action (call, email, meeting) based on lead profile and sales cycle stage
  • M5
    Compliance & Intent Validation
    Verify lead legitimacy and compliance status against blocklist and regulatory requirements
Function 2 · Opportunity Development & Pipeline Management
Sales Management & Analytics
Provides management visibility into pipeline health and deal risk early enough for coaching interventions without AI-driven automation replacing judgment.
  • M6
    Pipeline Health Dashboard
    Aggregate deal metrics and flag stalled opportunities or velocity anomalies by segment and sales rep
  • M7
    Deal Risk Assessment
    Estimate deal slippage probability and likely close date variance based on historical patterns and engagement pace
  • M8
    Revenue Forecast Support
    Generate monthly and quarterly revenue projections with confidence intervals by product and account segment
  • M9
    Sales Rep Performance Benchmark
    Analyze individual and team win rates, cycle time, and deal size against peer and historical baselines

Module Task Classifiers
F1 Sales Operations Management T1–T6 Classifier Tasks
T1T1 [Scope]: Sales Ops receives request to auto-sync contact updates from LinkedIn Sales Navigator into Salesforce daily. Assessment: Agentic AI (Function 2 territory) masquerading as Ops task. Resolution: Clarify: is this a one-time ETL (Ops) or intelligence-driven sync (Analytics)? Route to correct owner.
T2T2 [Risk]: Ops team wants to use unvetted free AI tool to auto-generate sales collateral templates. Assessment: Tool fragmentation risk + IP/brand loss. Resolution: Sandbox test within Claude API first, build prompt library, lock template approval gate.
T3T3 [Integration]: CRM admin proposes AI-powered lead assignment logic. Assessment: Touches quota allocation (Function 2 Analytics authority). Resolution: Co-own with Sales Management; Ops implements, Analytics validates fairness + defines success KPIs.
T4T4 [Scaling]: Ops rolls out Claude API integration to 50-person team; 10 users adopt, 40 ignore. Assessment: Change management gap; no playbook. Resolution: Identify 5 power users, build case study, run train-the-trainer, measure velocity lift, iterate.
T5T5 [Compliance]: Ops asks if call transcripts can be fed to ChatGPT for deal summaries. Assessment: PDPA risk. Resolution: No raw transcripts; anonymize first, use Claude system prompts to enforce no-PII rules, audit log every call.
T6T6 [Governance]: Ops and Analytics disagree on who owns the shared AI prompt library. Assessment: Ownership ambiguity. Resolution: Ops owns library infrastructure (versioning, access control); Analytics owns sales-domain prompt quality & updates; bi-weekly sync.
F2 Sales Management & Analytics T1–T6 Classifier Tasks
T1T1 [Discovery]: Analytics manager sees ChatGPT keyword demand (9/19 keywords) and recommends ChatGPT for forecasting. Assessment: Tool selection without use-case clarity. Resolution: Map forecasting needs (historical accuracy, explainability, integration) against tools; Claude may outperform ChatGPT for structured CRM data.
T2T2 [Data]: Analytics wants to build AI-powered pipeline forecast using raw customer names + deal amounts. Assessment: PII risk + model drift risk. Resolution: Use anonymized deal features (stage, days-in-cycle, company size, vertical, rep tenure); predict outcomes, not customer identity.
T3T3 [Validation]: AI model predicts top 20 deals likely to close in Q3; sales team disputes predictions on 8 deals. Assessment: Model interpretability gap; team doesn't trust black box. Resolution: Require Claude to show reasoning (e.g., 'deal stalled 45 days, competitor mentioned, discount asked = 35% risk'). Rebuild trust through explainability.
T4T4 [Scaling]: Pilot AI analytics with 1 team (12 people); success (15% forecast accuracy lift). Roll out to 5 teams (60 people); adoption drops to 30%. Assessment: Playbook didn't scale; onboarding too heavy. Resolution: Build 2-hour demo, record walkthrough, assign peer champion per team, measure adoption at 4 & 8 weeks, iterate messaging.
T5T5 [Governance]: Sales Management and Finance disagree on AI forecast authority—whose number goes to board? Assessment: Accountability gap. Resolution: AI forecast feeds Sales Management (tactical), not board (Finance owns official guidance); Sales Mgmt accountable for variance analysis monthly.
T6T6 [Ethics]: Analytics team uses AI to predict which reps are flight risks and flags low performers. Assessment: Fair-use red flag; reps may feel surveilled, may trigger attrition. Resolution: Use AI for team health (workload, deal velocity) not individual surveillance; share insights with reps as coaching tool, not performance judgment.

Three Strategic Dilemmas
01
Productivity Paradox
Time to train staff on AI workflows vs. immediate revenue pressure to close deals without delay
→ Run parallel tracks: assign 20% of team to AI pilot (low-risk accounts) while core group maintains quota. Pilot team ramps faster, wins become case studies driving adoption. No revenue hit; 3-month payoff.
02
Data Privacy vs. Personalization
Using customer data to train AI models for hyper-personalized outreach risks PDPA violations and customer trust erosion
→ Adopt zero-PII prompting discipline: feed AI only anonymized summaries (company size, industry, deal stage) not customer names, contact details. Use Claude's system prompts to enforce guardrails. Maintain audit log of every AI-generated message for compliance.
03
Tool Fragmentation
Each function (Sales Ops, Sales Analytics) picks different AI tools, creating skill silos, integration debt, and cost sprawl
→ Standardize on 1–2 core AI platforms (e.g., Claude API + ChatGPT for breadth) with clear handoff workflows. Sales Ops owns function 1 (CRM automation), Sales Management owns function 2 (analytics dashboards). Shared prompt library and monthly knowledge-share sessions.
